About

Who we are

Glamp UP was formed in December 2022 by two Medical Laboratory Scientists, Brooke and Robert, and one lab mix named Copper. After traveling the U.S. coast-to-coast working contracts at various hospitals, we chose Little Lake, MI as our place to call home, choosing it for its abundance of nature, clear night skies, and mountain biking trails in nearby Marquette, MI.

What we are

We are building luxury glamping units on forty acres of wooded land in Little Lake, MI with access to 900 acres of state land and beachfront on a small private lake. Our focus is on sustainability, off-grid living, and the enjoyment of nature withought compromising on comfort. Our units will be fully powered by off-grid solar, and will feature composting toilets. Tentativley we will have our cabin and one luxury tent available by the summer of 2023 and plan to have an additional luxury tent and two geodesic domes by late spring 2024.

Glamping

Cabin

Enjoy this spacious 380 sqft cabin that sits right on the lake. Amenities include a queen size bed, solar-powered lights, and a propane heater and stove.

Domes

Stay in one of our 195 sqft glamping domes. Stargaze through the large skylight and wake up to beautiful nature views out the expansive bay window. - Coming 2023

Tents

Not your typical tent. These spacious tents feature a queen size bed, countertops, and comfy chairs to relax in after a day of mountainbiking or 4-wheeling. - Coming 2024

Outdoor Activities

Mountain Biking

Thirty minutes from the mountainbiking trails of Marquette, Michigan.

Kayaking and SUPing

Cool off with a swim or relax with some kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding on ten acre Northwestern lake via our private beachfront access. Be sure not to miss the beautiful sunset over the lake in the evening!

Hiking

In addition to the beautiful hiking trails throughout the area, Glamp U.P. borders over 900 acres of stateland with miles of trails to explore starting right from your glamping unit.

Skywatching

Expansive sky views and little light pollution make the Upper Peninsula the perfect place to view meteor showers or catch a glimpse of the Northern Lights.

Foraging

Explore the wonderful world of finding food. The surrounding forests are brimming with wild blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, and fantastic fungi.
